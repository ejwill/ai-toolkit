# MakerWorld Images

## When to use

Use this skill when planning or editing MakerWorld listing images, covers, gallery photos, comparison shots, or GIFs.

## Workflow

1. Choose the image types needed for the listing.
2. Plan the gallery order around clarity and fit.
3. Keep the subject large, readable, and well-lit.
4. Export clean final assets for the target listing formats.

## Core Rules

- Make the first image answer "what is this?" immediately.
- Use the cover image as a crop-first problem, not just a photo.
- Show the model on the real product whenever possible.
- Use detail shots to show print quality, texture, or labeling.
- Use comparison shots to clarify variants or print modes.
- Use GIFs only when motion adds information.

## Common Checks

- The hero image reads well on mobile.
- The app cover still works when cropped vertically.
- At least one image proves fit and scale.
- The gallery has a story and not just repeated angles.
- The final exports are clean and sized for the listing platform.
